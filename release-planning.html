Release Planning

Everything a new team member — or a partner from another team — needs to understand how Release Planning works: what it owns, who does what, how work comes in, and how a release moves from internal approval to shipped product, digital and physical, US and international.

01
What Release Planning Owns

The operational engine that turns an approved release into scheduled, cleared, manufactured and delivered product. The mandate, in plain terms.

Digital Delivery
Every digital asset — audio (stereo, Atmos, 360RA), artwork, metadata, video — cleared and delivered to DSPs accurately and on schedule.
GRPS Setup & Scheduling
Set up, manage and schedule releases in GRPS once senior management approves. The system of record for what ships when.
Rights & Clearance
Coordinate with A&R Admin, Business Affairs and IPL/Copyright so samples, side-artist and rights are cleared before release.
Physical — US
Manufacturing timelines with U.S. Supply Chain — costs, mastering, test pressings, packaging, on-time delivery to plants.
Physical — Ex-US
International product through European Services and Int'l Marketing — territory versions, forecasting and local manufacturing.
High-Security Releases
Controlled delivery cadence, watermarking and confidentiality on sensitive projects to minimize leak risk.

03
How Work Comes In

Release Planning is triggered by internal approval. Nothing is set up in GRPS until senior management signs off.

A release is confirmed internally and approved by senior management — that is the trigger. Release Planning then independently sets it up in GRPS and becomes the operational owner: sequencing clearance, asset delivery, manufacturing and scheduling across every partner team. For physical, the same confirmation kicks off coordination with U.S. Supply Chain or European Services. From there it moves through the lanes below.
