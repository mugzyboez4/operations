JD Discrepancies — Campaign Lead (Shani, 2026-03-22) vs. Meeting Decisions
7 gaps between the written JD and what Tarek confirmed on April 3. These need resolution before the JD is final.
1. "Audience Development Integration" overstates CL authority
JD says: CL core responsibility — "Audience Development Integration." Implies CL drives integration with Aud Dev.
Meeting says: Aud Dev operates independently on execution. CL sets budget + can request specific things, but Aud Dev owns their own strategy (definition, growth, engagement). Tarek: "if audience development feels like they could do more with more, then that's a conversation."
Impact: JD overstates CL authority. Real relationship = CL briefs the objective, Aud Dev decides how.
2. "OOH Media" — not on the board at all
JD says: "OOH Media" listed as a core CL responsibility.
Meeting says: Never mentioned in 31 minutes. Not on the lane board. Zero discussion.
Impact: Orphan responsibility — either clearly CL-owned (no debate needed) or a holdover from old structure. Needs confirmation.
3. "Budget Alignment and Distribution" vs. "Budget Oversight & Strategy"
JD says: "Budget Alignment and Distribution" — implies CLs parcel out budget to other teams.
Meeting says: Tarek reframed as "budget oversight and strategy." CLs set the budget available to Aud Dev, who executes within it. There's a "feedback element" — not just CL deciding.
Impact: "Distribution" suggests more hands-on control than Tarek described. Language mismatch.
4. Three new CL responsibilities missing from JD
JD says: No mention of triggering assets, paid ads (Gupta), or timeline management as distinct responsibilities.
Meeting says: Tarek added all three — Timeline Creation & Management, Triggering Core Campaign Assets (pre-saves, websites, marketing assets), Paid Ads (Gupta Campaigns). These are net new CL scope.
Impact: JD is missing 3 responsibilities Tarek explicitly assigned. "Campaign Strategy, Planning & Timelines" partially covers timeline, but triggering assets and paid ads are absent.
5. "Interdepartmental Alignment" — unresolved overlap with Campaign Ops
JD says: "Campaign Leadership and Interdepartmental Alignment" as core CL responsibility.
Meeting says: Campaign Ops also exists as the cross-department coordination layer. The implicit line: CL aligns on strategy/narrative, Ops aligns on execution/tracking/follow-through. But nobody stated this explicitly.
Impact: Two functions claim "interdepartmental alignment." The JD doesn't distinguish CL alignment from Ops alignment.
6. JD still references "Digital" as a department
JD says: CL "aligns efforts across Audience Development, Digital, Publicity, A&R, Creative, Commerce, and International teams."
Meeting says: Tarek was emphatic — "I definitely don't want to be calling anything a digital campaign unless it's something the audience development team is running." The restructuring eliminated "Digital" as a separate function.
Impact: JD uses pre-restructuring language. "Digital" should be removed from the department list. Aud Dev absorbed this function.
7. Roster upkeep & dissemination — no centralized ownership
JD says: Each CL JD includes a printed roster, but there's no defined process for how roster changes get communicated across the company. Multiple CLs maintain separate rosters (Shani's vs. other CLs) with no single source of truth.
Proposal: Departments create and maintain their own one sheets and roster info, but route all updates through Campaign Ops. CO becomes the single point of dissemination — no dept-to-dept roster updates bypassing Ops. CO tracks changes and ensures the rest of the company has current information.
Impact: Without this, roster changes propagate unevenly — some depts find out late, others never. CO should own the system of record for roster assignments and be the routing layer for any updates.
