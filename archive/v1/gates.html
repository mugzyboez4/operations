CAMPAIGN
GATES

RCA Records · Campaign Operations · April 2026
Nothing moves forward until the gate is clear. Every campaign starts with the full scope. Items may be removed only by explicit decision — tier, budget, or strategy. If a gate isn't met, the release doesn't advance.
Gate 1
Strategy Locked
Before any assets are created or dates are discussed
Must be true
Campaign narrative defined — CL has the story, the angle, the audience approach
Artist & management aligned — buy-in confirmed, not assumed
Budget framework built — even rough, allocation exists across departments
Creative briefed — art direction, video needs, press image direction communicated
Who owns the checkpoint
Campaign Lead confirms. Campaign Ops verifies it exists in the system.
Gate 1 clear → assets and scheduling can begin
Gate 2
Schedule Ready
Before a release date is submitted for executive approval
Must be true
Art clearance confidence — reasonably confident artwork will clear by deadline
Final master on track — audio will be delivered ≥10 days before release
Music circulated — at minimum, SMT and core team have heard the track
Plan exists — even loose, there's a document that says what happens and when
Audience Dev activated — assignment made based on genre, bandwidth, and campaign needs
Who owns the checkpoint
Campaign Lead initiates. Campaign Ops checks prerequisites and submits the scheduling request. Executive team approves or denies at Friday meeting.
Gate 2 clear → date goes on the schedule
Gate 3
Assets Delivered
7–10 days before release
Must be true
Final artwork — square, clean, social crops, thumbnails delivered
Press images — portraits, DSP photos, credits and clearance confirmed
Video asset — visualizer or video delivered and cleared through Earworm
Short form clips — vertical cuts, BTS, lyric/motion clips ready
Campaign docs — lyrics, boilerplate, pitch quotes, asset credits, central folder live
Digital rollout plan — tease, announcement, release week, sustain mapped out
Owners assigned — every asset type has a named person responsible
Who owns the checkpoint
Campaign Ops runs the readiness check. Campaign Lead resolves gaps. If gaps remain, they're documented with rationale — the Reduction Log.
Gate 3 clear → release week execution begins
Gate 4
Release Week
Day 0 through Day +7
Must be true
Day 0 content live — "out now" post, video/visualizer, link-in-bio updated
Platform coverage confirmed — TikTok, IG Feed, IG Stories, YouTube Shorts
Day +1–3 content queued — alt clips, personal captions, fan reaction reposts
Sustain plan active — BTS, UGC reposts, acoustic/live content, focus-track reframing
Who owns the checkpoint
Audience Dev executes the rollout. Campaign Lead monitors narrative. Campaign Ops confirms coverage is happening and flags drops.
Gate 4 clear → campaign enters sustain
Gate 5
Campaign Close
2–4 weeks post-release (or when next project begins)
Must be true
Performance reported — streaming, social, paid media results documented
Budget reconciled — actual spend vs. approved budget logged
Learnings captured — what worked, what didn't, what to do differently
Reduction log filed — if anything was removed from scope, the reason is on record
Who owns the checkpoint
Campaign Ops runs the close-out. Audience Dev delivers performance report. Campaign Lead signs off.
Gate 5 clear → campaign archived, learnings available for next project
