Where It Crosses Over

Creative × Campaigns

Campaigns decides what the campaign says and when. Creative decides how it looks and gets made. Almost every Creative responsibility is the back half of something Campaigns starts — which is why they feel like one team. These are the seams where the baton passes, and where work drops if the handoff is fuzzy.

■ Campaigns — strategy, narrative, timeline, budget, the ask ■ Creative — concept, craft, production, delivery
The Brief
Trigger: campaign needs an asset
Campaigns
Sets strategy & narrative; triggers the assets the campaign needs.
Brief in →
assets out
⇄
Creative
Turns the brief into finished work — shoots, videos, art, decks.
Ops watch · Did a real brief land, with enough to start? A vague brief is the #1 source of rework.

Paid Ad Assets
Three-way handoff
Campaigns
Owns ad strategy and the brief.
Brief → assets
→ paid media
→
Creative
Produces the ad assets → then Audience Dev runs the paid media.
Ops watch · Assets finished and approved before the flight date — the seam where Creative and Aud Dev both touch it.
Also crosses on: pitch decks (Campaigns owns the ask → Design builds), physical product (Campaigns confirms config → Design packages → Support delivers), promo items, and award-show assets. Same shape every time — Campaigns owns the ask, Creative builds the artifact.
On combining the two teams: keep each team's lanes intact and let this handoff layer be the seam — don't melt Creative and Campaigns into one column set. Two clear boards joined at the handoffs reads as partnership; one merged column set reads as a takeover, and reintroduces the turf anxiety we're trying to defuse. Campaign Ops watches these seams; it doesn't own them.
