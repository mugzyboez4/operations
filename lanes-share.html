Key Processes
Release Scheduling
1.Campaign Lead identifies target release date
2.CL confirms with Campaign Ops — CO checks the landscape
3.CO submits scheduling request (requires: art clearance confidence, final master on track, music circulated to SMT, rough plan in place)
4.Reviewed at Friday executive scheduling meeting
5.If approved → entered into GRPS, shows on Release Runway
6.If denied → CO communicates back to CL with reasoning
No date is confirmed to artists or management until approved by the executive team.
Paid Ads
CLOwns strategy and provides ad assets (artwork, clips, copy)
ADExecutes campaigns — targeting, optimization, platform-native delivery
Budget requests must include target audience and goals — "10K for influencer and 10K for seeding" without audience detail will not be approved.
Budget Reallocation
1.Campaign Lead decides funds need to shift (e.g., promo appearances → digital advertising)
2.CL directs Support to make the transfer in Console
3.Support executes the admin task — they do not initiate transfers on their own
4.Campaign Ops tracks for budget visibility
Creative Asset Requests
1.Campaign Lead provides brief to Support (what's needed, dimensions, timeline)
2.Support enters request into Airtable with correct specs
3.Support tracks delivery and routes for approval
4.Campaign Ops tracks overall asset readiness against release deadlines
Music Circulation
1.Once music is ready or a release is being scheduled, CL flags with A&R Admin
2.Music is circulated to internal teams via standard distribution list
3.External music approvals handled separately through existing process
Standard distribution lists are maintained by Support. One-off requests go through CL.
Profile & Channel Setup
ADAudience Dev decides strategy — what the bio says, what the featured image is, platform-specific positioning
SUPSupport executes updates across all social and DSP profiles to ensure consistency
Whatever is updated on Instagram should be reflected across all DSPs and platforms.
Additional departments — Data & Analytics, Commercial Partnerships, Creative, and other departments will be mapped separately and added to this document as those conversations are completed.
