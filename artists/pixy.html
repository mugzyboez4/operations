Unsigned Artist Evaluation

PIXY

Dark Alt-Pop Brazil Alt-Electronic Unsigned
Giovanna Rodrigues Tomaz is a Brazilian solo artist releasing dark alt-pop under the name PIXY (formerly Pixxy Lua). She writes on every track, has released 10+ singles and a debut EP since 2023, and works with a rotating cast of independent producers. The music sits in a lane between daine, PureSnow, and Nokia Angel—atmospheric, emotionally raw, and built for playlist discovery. She’s very early stage with a small but growing social footprint, and there are real discoverability issues to solve before anything can scale.

Market Context
Why Now

Dark alt-pop is one of the most active playlist-discovery lanes right now, with artists like daine, Nokia Angel, and PureSnow building audiences primarily through algorithmic placement and editorial pickup. Brazil is also an increasingly valuable origin market for global pop acts—but most of that pipeline has been Latin pop, funk carioca, or sertanejo. A Brazilian artist writing in English in the dark alt-pop space is unusual and potentially interesting as a crossover play. That said, PIXY is extremely early stage. Social numbers are tiny, there’s no press footprint, no visible management, no live history, and no distribution deal on record. The XY EP just dropped March 18, 2026, which means we’re seeing this at the earliest possible window. The question isn’t whether the music is ready—it’s whether there’s enough here to justify an artist development bet before someone else spots it.

Sound & Comparables
Musical DNA

PIXY makes dark, atmospheric alt-pop with electronic production and emotionally raw lyrics. The writing gravitates toward self-destruction, isolation, and performative darkness. Production ranges from hyperpop-adjacent beats (badbadbad, Daniel Angell) to more restrained alt-electronic (Calvin Young, Jacob Wice). Consistent vocal identity across multiple producer partnerships. Titles skew dramatic and anime-influenced (SHINIGAMI, DEMÔNITA, LEGACY). She writes on everything.

A&R AdministrationHigh
▼

Multiple producer/songwriter collaborators across the catalog means clearing existing rights will be a factor. Releases span at least three different production teams (Arthur Besna, Calvin Young/Jacob Wice, Daniel Angell/Beau Miles-Watson/badbadbad). → VERIFY: Current distribution arrangement for existing catalog—DistroKid? TuneCore? Existing masters ownership?

Business & Legal AffairsCritical
▼

Two major legal considerations. First: the name PIXY collides with a disbanded K-pop girl group (PIXY/픽시) that still holds 514K monthly Spotify listeners and whose former members are now forming a new group (AWU). Trademark exposure is real. Second: if PIXY is Brazilian-based, visa/work authorization logistics for any US-based activity need early scoping. This is an artist development deal at most—likely small advance, optioned project commitment.

→ VERIFY: Is she represented by an attorney or manager? Any existing label conversations?

→ VERIFY: Current country of residence—Brazil or relocated?
