Campaign Ops × Campaign Leads

Two functions, one campaign. Ops sees across departments; Leads drives within them. This is the line — by operating language, by phase, by meeting, and by what neither side does. Read this first; the workflows below assume it.

Project RolloutCampaign Ops sees across departments. Campaign Leads drives within them.
  • Surface cross-department readiness to Active Projects
  • Flag blockers visible across departments

  • Confirm all Runway fields populated
  • Own the rollout plan end-to-end
  • Execute press, radio, content, DSP strategy

  • Own all external partner communications
Release WeekMinimal — Campaign Leads runs the release. Campaign Ops monitors and escalates.
  • Track real-time performance (Day 1, Week 1)
  • Surface early signals to leadership
  • Flag deviations from projections
  • Execute all launch-week tactics
  • Own day-of communications and war room
  • Own all partner and platform relationships
Post-ReleaseCampaign Ops provides the retrospective. Campaign Leads owns what's next.
  • Surface post-release analytics to Active Projects
  • Track catalog performance and playlist retention
  • Flag opportunities (sync, international, brand)
  • Prompt next-phase planning conversations
  • Execute sustain strategy (content, touring, features)
  • Own artist relationship through dormancy
  • Build case for next project investment
DormantCampaign Ops alerts when data moves. Campaign Leads decides when to re-engage.
  • Track catalog health passively
  • Surface signal spikes (sync placement, viral moment)
  • Flag re-engagement opportunities
  • Maintain artist relationship
  • Own decision to re-engage or hand back

The Line — What Neither Side Does
Campaign Ops Never…
Campaign Leads Never…
Communicates directly with artist management on campaign decisions
Sees the full cross-department readiness picture simultaneously
Approves or denies a creative direction

Runs the post-meeting analysis or three-pass transcript review
When a decision gets made in one room, every other team knows what it means for them — and nothing falls through the cracks. That's Campaign Ops. The team that turns that knowledge into action on the artist's behalf? That's Campaign Leads.
