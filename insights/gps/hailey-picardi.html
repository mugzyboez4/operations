5 Things to Know

01The audience is ahead of the music. IG engagement at 23% (bench 4.9%) and TikTok like-to-view at 22.7% (bench 10.4%) are both top 1% of all artists in the dataset. The person is generating heat. The catalog is not converting it — catalog momentum sits at P17.
02Youngest audience on the roster. Core streaming age 20.8, core socials age 19.6 — both 99th+ percentile. 14% of Spotify listeners are 13–17 (index 262 vs benchmark). 53% of IG followers are 18–24. This is a Gen Z artist with a Gen Z audience.
03Only 18 weeks of streaming data. She’s brand new. 4-week US average of 821K is solid for an artist this early, but the trajectory is still being written. Peak week hit 1.28M in late February.
0484.8% female audience on Spotify (index 177). The male index is 24 — among the most female-skewing profiles in the dataset. Brand and sync targeting should reflect this.
05Over-indexes heavily in rural/small-market US: Clarksburg WV (238), Terre Haute IN (231), Rochester MN (222). Internationally: Norway (299), Ireland (290), Great Britain (193). The UK/Ireland corridor is her strongest international lane.

Age Distribution

The streaming audience is young but the IG audience is even younger. On Spotify, 18–22 (31.6%, index 158) and 13–17 (14.1%, index 262) dominate. On Instagram, 18–24 is 53% of followers and 13–17 over-indexes at 385. The social audience is a younger subset of the streaming audience — the content is reaching teens who haven’t fully converted to streaming yet.

Gender — Spotify (WW)

One of the most female-skewing profiles in the dataset. 84.8% female on Spotify (index 177). Male listeners are an afterthought at 11.3%. This isn’t unusual for a young female pop artist, but the degree of skew is notable — it suggests the appeal is strongly identity-driven.
